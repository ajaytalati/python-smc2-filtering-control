\begin{abstract}
\noindent
These notes present the mathematical theory underlying a research code base
that combines particle filtering and stochastic optimal control on a
three-dimensional physiological stochastic differential equation. We define
the FSA-v2 model in its G1-reparametrized form, prove that all $30$ static
parameters are locally identifiable from a one-day window of four-channel
observations by analytical Fisher-information-matrix calculation, and
establish exponential ergodicity of the SDE on its operating domain via a
quadratic Lyapunov function. We then develop the bootstrap particle filter
and the SMC${}^2$ algorithm of Chopin, Jacob and Papaspiliopoulos,
recast optimal scheduling of training stimulus as posterior inference
under a tempered cost likelihood, and close the loop in a model-predictive
controller that interleaves a rolling SMC${}^2$ filter with a
posterior-mean control SMC${}^2$ over an RBF schedule. The receding-horizon
section is presented in both abstract mathematical and code-level form, and
includes a linear--quadratic--Gaussian / differential-Riccati alternative.
We then report the Stage~H multi-horizon comparison showing that no LQG
variant matches the SMC${}^2$ MPC on this plant -- the gap traceable to
three structural defects of the linearised state space -- and the Stage~I
confirmation that the SDE solver's outer step can be enlarged from $15$
to $60$ minutes for a $4\times$ wall-clock speedup with no qualitative
change in the control problem.

\medskip
\noindent
The audience is graduate students in applied mathematics, mathematical
engineering, applied stochastics, or process engineering. Familiarity with
It\^o stochastic differential equations, Bayesian filtering, and basic
optimal control at the level of a one-semester course is assumed; the
particle-filtering and tempered-SMC machinery is built up from first
principles.
\end{abstract}
