\begin{abstract}
\noindent
These notes present the mathematical theory underlying a research code base
that combines particle filtering and stochastic optimal control on a
three-dimensional physiological stochastic differential equation. We define
the FSA-v2 model, develop the bootstrap particle filter and the SMC${}^2$
algorithm of Chopin, Jacob and Papaspiliopoulos, recast optimal scheduling
of training stimulus as posterior inference under a tempered cost likelihood,
and close the loop in a model-predictive controller that interleaves a
rolling SMC${}^2$ filter with a posterior-mean control SMC${}^2$ over an
RBF schedule. We end by describing how the abstract receding-horizon loop
is realised in code, by setting out a linear--quadratic--Gaussian
approximation that could be added as a cheap baseline, and by flagging
two outstanding analyses (Fisher information rank and Lyapunov stability)
that have not yet been carried out for the model.

\medskip
\noindent
The audience is graduate students in applied mathematics, mathematical
engineering, applied stochastics, or process engineering. Familiarity with
It\^o stochastic differential equations, Bayesian filtering, and basic
optimal control at the level of a one-semester course is assumed; the
particle-filtering and tempered-SMC machinery is built up from first
principles.
\end{abstract}
