\section{Identifiability via the Fisher information matrix}\label{sec:fim}

A model is no use if its parameters cannot be recovered from the
observations: an inference procedure may converge confidently to one
of an infinite family of indistinguishable parameter vectors. This
section asks whether the FSA-v2 model defined in
Section~\ref{sec:fsa} suffers from such a pathology, settles the
matter analytically, and reports the empirical fingerprint of the
diagnosis. The detailed derivations of the rank-deficient parameter
pairs and of the G1 reparametrization are deferred to
Appendix~\ref{app:fim}.

\subsection{Fisher information and local identifiability}\label{sec:fim-def}

For a parameter vector $\theta \in \R^d$ and an observation record
$y$, the score function is
$s(\theta;y) = \nabla_\theta \log p(y\mid\theta)$ and the
\emph{Fisher information matrix} (FIM) is
\begin{equation}
  \Ical(\theta) \;=\;
    \E_{y \sim p(\cdot\mid\theta)}\!\bigl[\,s(\theta;y)\,s(\theta;y)^{\!\top}\bigr].
  \label{eq:fim-def}
\end{equation}
$\Ical(\theta)$ is symmetric positive semidefinite, and two facts
about it explain why it is the right diagnostic.

\begin{proposition}[Local identifiability]\label{prop:fim-id}
Suppose $\theta \mapsto p(y\mid\theta)$ is twice differentiable in a
neighbourhood of $\theta^*$. Then $\theta^*$ is locally identifiable
if and only if $\Ical(\theta^*)$ has full rank $d$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Cram\'er--Rao]\label{prop:fim-cr}
Any unbiased estimator $\hat\theta$ of $\theta$ has covariance bounded
below in the positive-semidefinite ordering by $\Ical(\theta)^{-1}$
(when $\Ical$ is invertible).
\end{proposition}

In words: the FIM \emph{rank} tells you whether the data can pin
$\theta$ down at all, and the FIM \emph{inverse} tells you how
sharply. A rank-deficient FIM means there is a direction in
parameter space along which the data are silent -- two distinct
$\theta$ vectors produce indistinguishable observation distributions
-- and any procedure trying to estimate $\theta$ in that direction
will be at the mercy of the prior, not the data.

\subsection{The pre-G1 model has three rank-deficient pairs}\label{sec:fim-deficient}

The four-channel observation model
\eqref{eq:obs-hr}--\eqref{eq:obs-steps} probes the latent state
$(B,F,A)$ through linear-in-state likelihoods, and the latent state
in turn carries information about the dynamics parameters via the
drift \eqref{eq:fsa-B}--\eqref{eq:fsa-A}. At a one-day window the
slow Banister state moves only marginally, so the inferential signal
about the dynamics parameters is concentrated in how
$\partial(B,F,A)/\partial\theta$ couples through the observation
likelihood.

A direct calculation along these lines (deferred to
Appendix~\ref{app:fim}) reveals that, near the operating point of
Definition~\ref{def:operating-point}, three pairs of pre-G1 parameters
enter the one-day likelihood through a single linear combination
each, so that the contribution of each pair to the FIM is rank-one
rather than rank-two. The three pairs and their identifiable
combinations are summarised in Table~\ref{tab:g1-reparam}.

The intuitive story behind each pair is the same: at one-day windows
the system rarely strays far from the operating point, so any
parameter whose effect manifests only via departures from that
operating point is invisible to the data. For instance, in the pair
$(\kappa_B, \varepsilon_A)$ the gain $\kappa_B$ multiplies the daily
training stimulus, while $\varepsilon_A$ multiplies $\kappa_B$ by a
factor close to one whenever $A \approx A_{\mathrm{typ}}$. Only the
\emph{product} $\kappa_B(1+\varepsilon_A A_{\mathrm{typ}})$ is felt by
the data; the individual factors are not.

\subsection{The G1 reparametrization absorbs the three pairs}\label{sec:fim-g1}

The cure is a simple coordinate change: rotate each rank-deficient
pair into a strongly identified linear combination and a residual
that vanishes at the operating point. The G1 reparametrization,
already shown in equations \eqref{eq:fsa-mu}--\eqref{eq:fsa-A},
performs this rotation. Table~\ref{tab:g1-reparam} lists the three
combinations side by side.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
Pair & Strongly identified & Residual & Vanishing condition \\
\midrule
$(\kappa_B,\varepsilon_A)$  & $\kappa_B^{\mathrm{new}} = \kappa_B(1+\varepsilon_A A_{\mathrm{typ}})$
                             & $\varepsilon_A$  & $A = A_{\mathrm{typ}}$ \\
$(\mu_F,\mu_{FF})$          & $\mu_F^{\mathrm{new}} = \mu_F + 2 F_{\mathrm{typ}} \mu_{FF}$
                             & $\mu_{FF}$       & $F = F_{\mathrm{typ}}$ \\
$(\tau_F,\lambda_A)$        & $\tau_F^{\mathrm{new}} = \tau_F/(1+\lambda_A A_{\mathrm{typ}})$
                             & $\lambda_A$      & $A = A_{\mathrm{typ}}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{The Stage~G1 reparametrization. Each rank-deficient parameter
pair of the pre-G1 model is rotated into a strongly identified
combination (left column) and a residual (middle column) that
vanishes at the operating point of
Definition~\ref{def:operating-point}. The parameter \emph{names} are
kept; only the meanings and truth values change. There is also an
implicit shift $\mu_0 \mapsto \mu_0 + \mu_{FF} F_{\mathrm{typ}}^2$
that absorbs the constant from completing the square in
\eqref{eq:fsa-mu}.}
\label{tab:g1-reparam}
\end{table}

The reparametrized drift is mathematically equivalent to the pre-G1
drift under a fixed relabelling of parameters; the equivalence
(stated as Proposition~\ref{prop:g1-equiv} in
Appendix~\ref{app:fim}) is verified numerically by a unit test in
\texttt{tests/test\_g1\_reparam.py} that confirms the two drift
formulas agree to floating-point precision over $50$ random states.

\subsection{Empirical evidence of identifiability after G1}\label{sec:fim-empirical}

The analytical argument predicts full FIM rank at the operating
point. The implementation furnishes empirical confirmation at
one-day rolling windows on simulated truth data.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lcc@{}}
\toprule
Quantity & Pre-G1 & Post-G1 \\
\midrule
$\rank \widehat{\Ical}$ at one-day window               & $d-3$            & $d$ \\
parameters covered by 90\,\% credible interval          & $27/30$          & $\mathbf{30/30}$ \\
identifiable estimable parameters                       & $3/6$            & $\mathbf{6/6}$ \\
posterior std on $\varepsilon_A$ (lognormal scale)      & $0.093$          & $\mathbf{0.016}$ \\
$90\,\%$ CI for $\varepsilon_A$                         & $[0.275,\,0.550]$ & $\mathbf{[0.373,\,0.423]}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Identifiability before and after the G1 reparametrization,
measured at one-day rolling-window scale on simulated truth data.
``Estimable'' counts the six parameters that the FIM analysis
identified as candidates for tightening. Numbers from
\texttt{version\_2/outputs/fsa\_high\_res/} and the Stage~G1 commit
record. The residual standard deviation on $\varepsilon_A$ tightens
by roughly a factor of six; analogous tightening is observed on the
other two residuals $\mu_{FF}$ and $\lambda_A$.}
\label{tab:g1-evidence}
\end{table}

The numbers in Table~\ref{tab:g1-evidence} confirm the analytical
prediction. The G1 form is identifiable at one-day windows; the
strongly identified parameters $(\kappa_B, \tau_F, \mu_F)$ retain
their original prior widths; and the residuals
$(\varepsilon_A, \lambda_A, \mu_{FF})$, whose posteriors were
formerly artificially wide because of the rank deficiency, now
contract to roughly a sixth of their pre-G1 spread under tightened
priors that the rank analysis itself motivates.

\begin{remark}[Where the FIM analysis lives in the code]\label{rem:fim-where}
The analytical FIM derivation summarised here -- and developed in
full in Appendix~\ref{app:fim} -- is recorded in the project's
commit log (Stage~G1) rather than as standalone code. A useful
follow-up would be to add a numerical FIM module under
\texttt{smc2fc/diagnostics/fim/} that reproduces these results from
the JAX dynamics by automatic differentiation, so that any future
modification of the model triggers a re-run of the rank check
automatically.
\end{remark}
